Top Events to Attend In Rome


1. Roman Carnival


The Roman Carnival is the biggest festivals in Italy. It is an ancient festival/tradition which dates back to the middle ages. It consists of a large public celebration that lasts for eight days, where the locals showcase horses, races, street theatre and dancing. This event ends the night of Mardi Gras (festivity where people celebrate the last night of a rich feast) and marks the beginning of lent (fasting period).


2. Romaeuropa


The is one of the biggest arts and culture festivals, which is held from September to November, where artists from all over the world come to sing and have a good time. It is where people of all ages comes to enjoy dance, theater, music andcircus acts.


3. Eurochocolate


This is another popular event which, even though was not celebrated by the Romans back then, is dedicated to, dare I say, chocolate. This is an event that used to take place in Perugia and has extended to places such as Rome. This event involves chocolate tasting, delightful food and different froms of entertainment.


4. Piazza Navona Christmas Market


Next on out list is the Piazza Navona Christmas Market, which as you might have guessed is not a festival at all. This is actually a famous Christmas market in Rome that takes place in the open space of Piazza Navona, where people can buy presents and get to go on the rides there.


5. Lungo il Tevere: Estate/Roman Summer Festival


The Lungo il Tevere, or the Roman Summer Festivial, is one event to witness! It is an annual event that is held from June to September that boasts an outdoor cinema, restaurants and a bar on the Island of Tiber along its river


6. Taste of Roma


This is event is for the food lovers out there. It is basically a giant food festival where you can experience and revel in some of Roma's finest cuisine made by the best chefs.


7.Città della Pizza


What would Roma or Italy be if there was not a festival of Pizza. This event event is held annualy on April and it involves enjoying the heavenly delicacy that is pizza from the fluffy Neapolitan pie to the thin Roman-crust pizza, made by some of the best pizza makers out there.


8. Fashion Shows


The final event on out list is fashion shows. Ask any fashionistas out there and they will tell you about the unique sense of style in Roma, or Italy as a whole. While fashions shows do not stem from ancient Roma, they are held every season to showcase some of the wildest and latest trends.
